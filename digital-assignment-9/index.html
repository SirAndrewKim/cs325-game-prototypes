Battle Dice

  • Originality: A game where you roll dice and see who has the highest roll to win.
  • Technical Merit: Lots of RNG. Clicking on the buttons to roll the die, that number is also associated with the image of the die.
  • Prototype Postmortem: Pairing an element of RNG with an image. I had a friend really help with the concept.
    And figuring out how to get everything to work was very interesting.
    The easiest part of associating the image to the number.
  • Prototype Assets: Using the analog concept from two weeks ago, but otherwise, the game is orginial.
    I edited the original dice to black for variance for player two.
    • Die art: Dice
  • Prototype Closest Other Game: This game is original.
  • High Concept: Pray to RNGesus!
  • Theme: Competitiveness. Battle. Strategies.
  • Mandated Variety: Mouse/Clickable buttons. A lot of randomness, fighting.
  • Prototype Goal: RNG. I wanted to work with something regarding dice rolls. But due to other class work loads, I never
    had time to really research more into how dice would work in Phaser.
  • Player Experience Goals: Competitiveness and overall fun. As this game is just a simple who has the higher roll.
  • Gameplay: This is a two player game. Each player rolls a die one at a time and whom ever has the highest value is the winner.
    In its current state, I wanted to have the concept of attack, defense, multiply like in my analog game.
    However, due to finals and my second covid dosage shot. I have not had much time to invest into this game.
    I will definitely be coming back to complete this after finals because this seems like a quick and fun game to play with friends.
  • Strategies: Unfortuantely, there is not counting cards when it comes to dice. Because this game is digital,
    how the dice is rolled is not effected by physical contact. So you can't manipulate how the dice falls.
  • Story/Setting/Premise: A setting similar to yugioh, but instead of cards, we are using Dice. Dueling your friends to see who is the better dice roller.
  • Target Audience: Anyone teenager and above!
  • Play Time: A few seconds to minutes.
